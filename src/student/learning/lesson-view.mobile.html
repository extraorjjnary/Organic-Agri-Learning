Understanding Compost Layers

Think of your compost pile like a lasagna. For the best results, you need to balance "Greens" (Nitrogen) and "Browns" (Carbon) in distinct layers to encourage healthy microbial activity.

1

The Foundation (Browns)

Start with 4-6 inches of coarse materials like twigs or straw. This provides essential aeration at the bottom of the pile.

2

The Nitrogen Kick (Greens)

Add kitchen scraps, fresh grass clippings, or manure. These nitrogen-rich materials provide the energy for microbes to grow.

3

The Carbon Cover (Browns)

Cover greens with more browns (shredded paper, cardboard, or dry leaves) to keep moisture in and odors down.
